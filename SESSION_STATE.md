# Ainu Project Session State
## Last Updated: 2026-05-13

## Theme
**"The Shaved Word: How the Ainu Spoke to Their Gods"**
Central motif: INAW — sacred shaved wooden sticks as the Ainu's unique spiritual technology.
Tagline: "In Ainu culture, every act of taking from the world required a handmade prayer. This is that prayer."

## Artifacts (Final)
1. **Inaw** (Sacred Shaved Sticks) — the base spiritual technology
2. **Nusa-San** (Sacred Community Fence) — communal prayer dimension
3. **Ikupasuy** (Prayer Stick) — ceremonial evolution of the inaw principle
4. **Attush Robe** (Elm Bark Robe with Mon'yō) — the body as walking inaw
5. **Makiri** (Carved Knife) — the tool that creates the prayer, itself a prayer

## Theme Selection Process
- Agents debated multiple rounds. Eliminated: bear ceremony (event not motif), fire (not unique - Zoroastrians/Vedic Agni), patterns (surface-level), reciprocity (too abstract).
- Final winner: INAW — passes all 5 tests (concrete, pervasive, uniquely Ainu, artifact-friendly, compelling for oral exam).
- Artifact #5 changed from Itanki bowl to Makiri knife after JSTOR research review (stronger visual, better inaw connection, image from Upopoy museum).
