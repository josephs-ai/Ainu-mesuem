# Artifact 5 — Tonkori and OKI's Revival

---

The tonkori is a long, narrow plucked zither — five strings stretched over a carved wooden body — originally belonging to the Sakhalin Ainu. Its name is onomatopoeic: say it aloud, and you almost hear the sound. Used to accompany oral epics and ceremonies, the tonkori carries a *ramat* — its own living spirit, alive within the wood.

By the late 20th century, the tonkori had nearly vanished. Then OKI (Oki Kano), the son of an Ainu father and Japanese mother, picked it up. He mastered the instrument and did something radical: he brought it to the world. OKI blends traditional tonkori playing with dub, reggae, and world music — touring internationally across Europe, North America, and Asia, introducing global audiences to a people most had never heard of.

The Ainu are not a museum exhibit. They are here, still playing, still fighting.
