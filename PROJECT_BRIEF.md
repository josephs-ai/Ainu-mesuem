# Ainu Indigenous Culture - Digital Exhibit Project

## Assignment
Research the Ainu people and create a digital exhibit with:
- Introduction Board (500-600 words)
- 5 Artifacts (100-150 words each) that "tell a story" together
- Works Cited page

This is for a high school class (Spring 2026, Mrs. Little).

## Phase 1: Research & Planning
Agents must collaboratively decide:
1. What narrative/story the 5 artifacts will tell together
2. Which 5 specific artifacts to feature
3. How the artifacts connect to each other and to the essential questions about stories, identity, and culture

## Key Context
- The Ainu are indigenous to Hokkaido (Japan), Sakhalin, and the Kuril Islands
- The assignment's essential questions focus on how origin stories shape identity, how stories privilege/marginalize groups, and how stories can drive social change
- Artifacts should span categories like: tools, clothing, art, instruments, ceremonial objects, food-related items, written/recorded materials
- Students can go for breadth (5 artifacts representing whole culture) OR depth (5 artifacts focused on one element)

## Writing Style
- Clear, engaging, informative — appropriate for a high school student
- Cite all sources with URLs
- Original writing, no plagiarism

## File Structure
- `research.md` — compiled research findings
- `plan.md` — agreed narrative + artifact selections with rationale
- `intro_board.md` — Introduction Board (Phase 2)
- `artifact_1.md` through `artifact_5.md` — Individual artifacts (Phase 2)
