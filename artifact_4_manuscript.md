# Artifact 4 — *Ainu Shin'yoshū* (Manuscript by Yukie Chiri, 1922)

**Category:** Written / recorded material
**Creator:** Yukie Chiri (1903–1922)
**Exhibit Role:** The crisis point — how close the culture came to total extinction

---

In 1922, a book called *Ainu Shin'yoshū* — "Collection of Ainu Divine Songs" — was published in Japan. Its author, Yukie Chiri, had died months earlier. She was nineteen.

The Ainu had no indigenous writing system. Their civilization — history, law, cosmology, ethics — lived in oral epics called *kamuy yukar*, performed by elders from memory, sometimes for hours. By Chiri's era, that tradition was dying. The 1899 colonial law had banned Ainu language in schools; each elder who died took irreplaceable knowledge with them.

As a teenager, Chiri transcribed and translated these epics into Japanese — using the colonizer's tools to save her people's knowledge. She sent the completed manuscript to be typeset and never saw it published.

*Ainu Shin'yoshū* is now one of the most important records of Ainu oral literature in existence. She barely made it.
